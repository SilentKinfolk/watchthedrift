how does this work?

A Casio F-91W is a quartz watch: accurate, but it slowly drifts away from real time, and you rarely notice until it’s well off. This tells you exactly how far off it is, right now.

Point your phone’s camera at the watch. The time on the face is read on your device — no photo ever leaves it — and compared against an internet time reference (NTP-style, corrected for network round-trip) at the instant of capture. The answer is the offset, with an honest margin of error.

Because the watch shows only whole seconds, a single reading is good to about half a second — plenty to catch a drift of several seconds. Nothing is stored or logged: every measurement stands on its own.
